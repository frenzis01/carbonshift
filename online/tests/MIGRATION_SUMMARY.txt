✅ Fix #3: ALIGNED EMISSION CALCULATIONS (2026-03-30)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Problem**: Online schedulers used simplified emission formula (carbon * duration),
while carbonshift.py used detailed power model (carbon * duration / 3600 * 0.05).
This caused massive numerical differences (e.g., optimal=2 gCO2, online=77,400 gCO2),
making comparison meaningless.

**Solution**: Updated ALL schedulers and tests to use carbonshift.py's formula:
  emission = carbon[gCO2/kWh] * duration[s] / 3600 * 0.05kW = gCO2

**Files Modified**:
- online/heuristics.py (line 141-144)
- online/tests/test_stress.py (line 124)
- online/tests/test_optimal_comparison.py (lines 170, 203, 232)
- online/tests/test_heuristics.py (lines 280, 378, 393)
- online/tests/test_scheduler_comparison.py (line 57-58)
- online/tests/README.md (emission calculation documentation)

**Result**: Now optimal and online schedulers produce comparable values:
  Before: optimal=2 gCO2, online=77,400 gCO2 (38,700x difference!)
  After:  optimal=2 gCO2, online=3 gCO2 (1.5x difference - meaningful!)
